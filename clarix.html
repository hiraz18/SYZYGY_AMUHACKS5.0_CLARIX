Evidence-Based

Every feature rooted in science

Polyvagal Theory
Vagal Nerve Activation
The 4-7-8 breathing pattern directly stimulates the vagus nerve, shifting the body from sympathetic (fight/flight) to parasympathetic (rest/digest) dominance within 90 seconds. Dr. Andrew Weil, 2015.
5-4-3-2-1 Technique
Sensory Grounding
By engaging all five senses sequentially, this trauma-informed technique interrupts the amygdala's panic signal and re-anchors the prefrontal cortex. Proven effective in PTSD treatment protocols (VA PTSD Guidelines).
Cognitive Load Theory
One Decision at a Time
Sweller's (1988) research shows working memory can process only 7Â±2 chunks. During crisis, capacity drops to 2-3. Clarix's binary-question interface respects this hard limit to prevent decision paralysis.
Neuroacoustics
Binaural Beat Entrainment
Binaural beats at 40Hz (Gamma) and 10Hz (Alpha) have demonstrated measurable reduction in cortisol levels. The brain's frequency-following response mirrors the external beat. (Oster, 1973; Huang & Charyton, 2008).
Pattern Interrupt
Breaking Dissociation
NLP pattern interrupts and sudden high-contrast stimuli (the 0.5s black screen + resonant tone) disrupt the freeze response by creating a cognitive "circuit break," forcing re-engagement.
Cognitive Reframing
Temporal Distancing
"An hour from now, this will already feel smaller" activates temporal self-distancing (Grossmann & Varnum, 2011), reducing emotional intensity by engaging the reflective-self rather than the reactive-self.
Humor Therapy
Micro-Comedy Release
Brief moments of levity post-crisis trigger the release of endorphins and reduce cortisol. Berk et al. (1989) demonstrated 39% cortisol reduction after laughing. Clarix's optional micro-comedy module applies this precisely.
Momentum Theory
Small Wins Architecture
Breaking crisis response into max 3 steps leverages the "small wins" effect (Weick, 1984), where each completed micro-task releases dopamine, rebuilding agency and reducing learned helplessness.
Box Breathing
Tactical Breathing
Used by Navy SEALs and ER physicians, box breathing (4-4-4-4) activates the parasympathetic nervous system within 4 breath cycles. Measured reduction in heart rate variability stress markers within 5 minutes.
